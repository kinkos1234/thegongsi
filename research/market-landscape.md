# Retail Investor Research Tools — Market Landscape & Positioning Brief

*Prepared 2026-04-14. Inspired by fey.com's mid-tier positioning.*

## 1. Current Landscape — How Retail Actually Gets Info

Retail investors cluster into four segments, each with distinct tool stacks:

**Casual retail (>80% of accounts).** Broker app (Robinhood, Webull, Fidelity mobile) + Yahoo Finance + Google Finance. Crypto: CoinGecko, CoinMarketCap, Coinbase app. Pain: ad-laden, shallow, engagement-optimized. Rarely paid; occasional Motley Fool Stock Advisor ($99/$199).

**Active retail / swing traders.** TradingView ($0 → $14.95 → $29.95 → $59.95 → $199.95) + broker + Finviz, Benzinga Pro ($177+/mo), StockAnalysis.com Pro ($79/yr), fintwit. Need: charts, news velocity, unusual options/volume.

**Serious retail / DIY fundamental investors** — **fey.com's target.** Stack: Seeking Alpha Premium ($239–299/yr) + Simply Wall St ($120–258/yr) + Koyfin ($40–70/mo) + Stockopedia + SEC EDGAR + earnings calls. They pay 3–5 subs and still context-switch.

**Crypto-native.** Free: CoinGecko, CMC, DefiLlama, Dune, Arkham. Paid: Messari Pro (~$30/mo), Nansen (free → ~$150/mo → $1.5k/mo), Glassnode ($40–$800/mo), CryptoQuant. On-chain is the clearest pro-retail frontier.

**Institutional (context only).** Bloomberg ~$32k/user/yr; Refinitiv ~$22k; FactSet ~$12k; S&P Capital IQ ~$13k+. Locked behind compliance — retail cannot buy.

## 3. Gaps & Opportunities in the Mid-Tier

$20–$80/mo prosumer slot has real white space. Serious-retail complaints:

- **Tool fragmentation** — 3–5 subs to cover news + data + screening + charts + fundamentals.
- **Koyfin** — powerful but steep learning curve, "data without opinion."
- **Seeking Alpha** — opinion but inconsistent quality, paywall even for premium, aggressive billing UX.
- **Simply Wall St** — accessible but shallow.
- **TradingView** — best charts, weak on fundamentals/filings.
- **No AI layer** on most. Earnings summaries + 10-K Q&A + NL screening are rare. Fey + Finchat.io compete.
- **Non-US coverage** poor across $20–80/mo (Koyfin is exception).
- **Crypto + equities in one tool** essentially unsolved at prosumer tier.
- **Portfolio analytics** primitive — factor exposure, attribution, risk decomposition live institutional-only.
- **Filings workflow** (13F, insider flow, 8-K, proxy) fragmented across WhaleWisdom, OpenInsider, EDGAR.

## 4. Differentiation Vectors (Ranked by Defensibility)

1. **AI-native workflow** — LLM summaries, NL screeners, Q&A over full disclosure history, agentic DD, portfolio-aware Q&A.
2. **Unified multi-asset** — equities + ETFs + crypto + on-chain in one terminal. Nobody owns this.
3. **Design/UX** — scarce; Fey proves design alone drives WTP.
4. **International / small-cap** — Koyfin's moat; under-served at consumer prices.
5. **Specific workflow ownership** — DD memos, rebalancing, alerts, 13F tracking, thesis journal.
6. **Community layer** — fintwit is the actual feed; no tool integrates it well.
7. **Price point** — $30/mo sweet spot; $15/mo undercut or $50/mo bundle both viable.
8. **Data quality / real-time** — harder moat (licensing) but defensible paired with above.

## 6. Positioning Hypotheses

**A — "Fey for Crypto + Equities" (multi-asset prosumer).** Serious retail holding both. Wedge: one portfolio, one AI research layer, one NL screener across stocks + tokens + on-chain. Why now: ETF-era TradFi/crypto blur; Fey refuses crypto; Messari/Nansen refuse equities. **$39/mo.**

**B — "AI DD Copilot" (agentic research).** DIY investors writing thesis memos, tracking 20–50 names. Wedge: ticker → agent produces defensible DD memo (bull/bear, competitors, filings anomalies, insider flow, earnings deltas); portfolio-aware Q&A; thesis journal with re-check alerts. Why now: LLMs finally cheap/good for multi-doc synthesis; Fey's AI is summary-only, not agentic. **$49/$99/mo.**

**C — "International + Small-Cap Terminal" (geographic).** Non-US retail (EU, UK, India, SEA) + US investors fishing overseas. Wedge: Koyfin-depth global at Fey-price, locale-aware filings (DART, SEDAR, companies-house), non-English earnings translation, FX-adjusted portfolios. **$25/mo.**

**D — "Portfolio OS" (workflow wedge).** Serious retail with $100k+ who have data but lack analytics. Wedge: factor exposure, risk decomposition, tax-lot optimization, rebalancing sim, thesis tracking, alert graph. Plaid/SnapTrade plug-in; don't compete on charts/news. **$20/mo flat or 0.05% AUM.**

**Shortlist.** **A** has largest TAM + clearest Fey-shaped hole. **B** has highest defensibility. **Hybrid — ship B as wedge inside A — is the strongest single bet.**
